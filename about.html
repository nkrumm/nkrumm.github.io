Research Experience

Ph.D doctoral candidate in Evan Eichler's Laboratory, UW Genome Sciences 2010-Present
Expected graduation: June, 2014
Max Planck Institute for Human Development and Lifespan Psychology 2007-2008
Developed a Matlab/SAS bootstrap analysis for a mixed multiple-regression model for discovering effects of BDNF/COMT gene allele on performance in a backwards recall task. Also performed a functional correlation analysis on fMRI data across time spectra and groups (using FSL). Additional work included basic database maintenance and development of new database solutions for storing experimental results and subject data.
Fulbright student in FU-Berlin Department of Psychology (Dr. Arthur Jacobs)
Fulbright graduate fellowship to study in Berlin, Germany at the Free University in Berlin and to work with Dr. Arthur Jacobs in the Department of Psychology. The project focused on the MROM (Multiple Read-Out Model) computer simulation of word recognition. Specifically, the goal of my project was to improve how the MROM model responds to longer and complex words. In addition to improving the computational model, I collected and correlated data derived from these new variations with experimental data derived from testing human subjects. The majority of the work was done with MATLAB.
Woodrow Wilson Fellowship independent investigation 2003-2007
Undertook a case study of a university student who showed a specific developmental deficit in learning the spellings of words in order to understand the cognitive functions which are involved in spelling, especially their development. In addition, investigated the relationship between reading and spelling and their interaction throughout development. Designed and carried out behavioral timing and psychometric experiments with the subject, their family, and a set of control subjects in order to define the specific deficits present.
